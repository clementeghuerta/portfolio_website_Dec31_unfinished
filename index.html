e-commerce application

About

About: This project started out in a very rough stage, which shows I can take and lead a project in a direction of concept to finshed product. The concepts startted with an e-commerce #dailyui email. For those who do not know what that is it is a daily forum that sends you ideas to create a user interface.As I started this project, I decided to create a one of a kind branding with beautiful colors and a great UX, which showed a path of purchase.

Objective + proccess

Objective:Design a clean flow for an e-commerce store
Process:Step 1: Write down what I wanted from the app.Step 2:Write each option for each page down in different sections, which then formed each individual page. Step 3:Sketch the user interface with the options I had written down and then finalizing each sketch. Step 4: Design with Adobe Illustrator and choosing the best colors, which showed the same branding across as well as a appealing design for beer. Step 5:Prototype with inVision. Step 6:Re-adjust the design. Step 7:Finalize all of the pages.

Infographics

About

About: Infographics are very fun to create and take much time and research. I believe that the main goal I set out to accomplish was accomplished.After a few weeks of research for each infographics, I wrote down ideas and sketched out possible ways of solving that problem.I decided to use three different data formats, which I think were used well for the type of data being showed to the reader.Each project took more than a month to complete and all together the projects tookjust over 3 months.

Objective + proccess

Objective: Create clean and highly informative interfaces
Process: concept and ideas: In this section of the process I conceptualized what I thought others like myself would like to learn about Apple. I had always wondered why their net worth is so high, so I thought to seek out why. Research: Research is alway a part of my design process and over the years I have gotten somewhat good at doing it. For this specific project, I mainly did stock related research and Apple tech specs reasearch also. Concept and layout of design: At this stage of the process, I sketched a couple of illustrations in thumnails then at a larger scale with side notes of what I thought needed more information.Final touches on Rough Draft Input from Others: I find myself, at times, a bit stuck on how to proceed with perfecting and symplifying my ideas to show information as clear as possible. For this project, I asked an art director to look at it and give me pointers.Final Clean-up: As for many designers, the last step in any design is to perfect everything, by which I mean make sure you are following the grid and branding across your design.

About me:

I love UI design, visual design, and graphic design. Some of my greatest inspirations come from great User Interfaces like the one's seen from Facebook and Apple. I love creating infographics and clean designs.
I am currently a graphic design student at California State East Bay studying graphic design and concentrating on UI Design. Before this, I attended Academy of Art University where I learned many different skills such as how to storyboard, edit video, and illustrate in 2D and 3D.
I love designing mobile first, using wireframes, researching, using stylescapes, creating user paths, prototyping, and designing responsive web designs (RWD), coding with: HTML5, CSS, CSS3, JavaScript(jQuery), and using Zurb Foundation, Bootstrap, and WordPress. Also, I have a passion in creating application (app) designs, and I am currently studying Human Interface Guidelines (GUI), Material Design and Lightning Design System.
Lastly, I have great visual, written, and verbal communication skills. I am very detail-oriented, resourceful, and well-organized. And I work great in self-direction and am also a great team player. And, I have a great love for typography.
